    Additional Information[edit]

    Main Article: Teleport
    • Alt shows your allied heroes' teleport status and cooldowns under their portraits below the top bar of the HUD.
      • If the ally has no teleport item, no icon appears.
      • The teleport status display has the following priorities: Boots of Travel 2 ➤ Boots of Travel ➤ Town Portal Scroll.
      • The teleport status does not show other teleport abilities (e.g. Teleportation).

    Properties[edit]

    Item Properties
    Ability
    Passive
    Affects
    Self
    All heroes start the game with an unsellable Town Portal Scroll.
    Initial Cooldown: 100
    Grants a charge whenever a hero dies to an enemy or an allied unit.

    The item gained this way neither has gold value nor affects the current Net Worth.

    These Town Portal Scroll are always consumed first upon cast.
    Charge Gain per Death: 1/0/0
    An unsellable Town Portal Scroll in a stack makes every item in the stack unsellable.
    This ability levels up withUpgrade: Town Portal Scroll.
    Details
      • Does not grant charges from the following death sources:
        • Self-denies.
        • Kill credited to neutral creeps.
      • Town Portal Scrolls gained this way does not refund gold when sold upon upgrading to Boots of Travel.
      • Heroes have a dedicated Town Portal Scroll slot on the HUD.
        • The item can neither be droppped nor can be carried in both the inventory or backpack.
      • Has the following interactions with Couriers:
        • Transports the item in their regular inventory.
        • Town Portal Scrolls in their inventory can be dropped and partially share them.

          • Enemy heroes can pick up the dropped item, making them the owner.

    Trivia[edit]

    • This item is based on the Scroll of Town Portal from Warcraft 3.
      • In DotA, the item is named Scroll of Town Portal as well.
    • On the August 13, 2018 Patch, a new Town Portal Scroll with a green icon made its way into the main game.
      • The item's active worked like the teleport of Boots of Travel 2, and costed only 50 .
      • The item was available in every game mode until it was fixed/removed one hour later in a hotfix patch.
      • The item's purpose is unknown, but it was likely intended to be part of Mutation Mode, due to being added in the same patch that added the Pocket Roshan item.
    • The description of the item is a reference to the very first Dota 2 Gamescom trailer.
    • Activating a Town Portal Scroll appears to require the casting hero's blood. This can be seen in the trailer, and in a scene the first episode Dota: Dragon's Blood, where Bram draws blood from his hand.
      • Dragon Knight's as Davion of Dragon Hold also draws blood from his hand during the custom teleport animation.
    • Within the pro scene, using Black King Bar before using Town Portal Scroll is sometimes refered to as "Nightfalling".
      • This is a reference to the pro-player Nightfall, who is known for using Black King Bar before teleporting, even if it seemed unnecessary.
