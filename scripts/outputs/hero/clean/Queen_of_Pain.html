    Bio

    [edit]
    They say pain is all in the mind, but they're wrong: It's all in my hands.
    ▶️  Akasha, the Queen of Pain
    Aliases:
    Akasha, QOP
    Lore:
    The Ecclesiast-King of Elze nursed a desire for pain — forbidden pain. In a less prominent political figure, such desires might be considered unwise, but in a monarch of his stature, to satisfy such thirsts would have threatened the virtue of the Divine Throne itself. Therefore he turned to his dungeon full of demonologists, promising freedom to whoever could summon a personal succubus of torment and bind it entirely to his service. The creature who arrived, Akasha by name, visited upon him such exquisite torments that he named her his Secret Queen, and he began to spend all his spare moments submitting to her clever torments—eventually abdicating all his responsibilities in his pursuit of the painful pleasures that only she could bring. Queen of Pain could bring him to the brink of death, but she was rune-bound to keep him alive. At last the King's neglect of state brought on an uprising. He was dragged from his chamber and hurled from the Tower of Invocations, and at the moment of death, Queen of Pain was let loose into the world, freed from servitude—freed to visit her sufferings on anyone she deigned to notice.
    Voice:
    Linda K. Morris (Responses)

    Trivia

    [edit]
    • "Akasha" translates to "aether" or "space" from Sanskrit.
      • In Hinduism and Neopaganism, Akasha (Aether or Space) is the 5th element within the Mahābhūta.
      • The five elements are represented by a pentagram, with Aether being the top point, as it is considered the fundamental element.
      • The other four elements are earth, water, fire, and air.
      • Note that this pentagram is different from the inverted pentagram, which has the 5th point facing down, not up.
    • Akasha's name may be inspired by the Queen of the Damned from the "Vampire Chronicles" novel series, who is named Akasha as well.
    • Queen of Pain's respawn response ▶️ "The Queen is dead, long live the Queen." is a reference to the saying "The King is dead, long live the King" which has been used throughout the centuries in the UK.
      • Her death response ▶️ "Your queen is dead." may be a reference to this as well.
    • Her Illusion Rune response ▶️ "Misery loves company." is a well-known proverb that originated over 400 years ago in Christopher Marlowe's "The Tragical History of the Life and Death of Doctor Faustus".
    • Her rivalry response towards Phantom Assassin▶️ "Blink! You're dead." is a reference to the Dr. Who episode "Don't Blink" in which the Doctor says, referring to Weeping Angels, "Don't blink, don't even blink. Blink, and you're dead."
    • Scream of Pain and Sonic Wave's ability sounds are based on some voice lines of Queen of Pain. Scream of Pain is based on this ▶️, while Sonic Wave uses a part of this ▶️.
    • Warcraft 3 similarities:
      • Queen of Pain uses the model of the Queen of Suffering, a big neutral creep.
        • Queen of Pain's title combines the names of the Queen of Suffering, with the Maiden of Pain.
      • Most of Queen of Pain's skillset was originally based on the Warden's skillset.
        • Shadow Strike's original iteration is identical to the Warden's Shadow Strike.
        • Blink's original iteration is identical to the Warden's Blink.
        • Scream of Pain's original iteration is identical to the Warden's Fan of Knives.
          • Prior to 6.00, Scream of Pain was named Fan of Knives as well, and had her throwing knives like the Warden.
      • Sonic Wave's original iteration is identical to the Tauren Chieftain's Shockwave.
