    Steals the spells of his enemies to use against them.
    A renowned duelist among sorcerers, Rubick ascended to the title of Grand Magus through the careful elimination of his rivals. Accustomed to assassins, Rubick reacts by lifting attackers off the ground with his mind using Telekinesis to fling them where he will. Arcane Supremacy amplifies his every spell, while enemy arms falter being struck with Fade Bolt. No magic is beyond the reach of the Grand Magus. Rubick replicates enemy specialties and their most unique advantage against them, Stealing and deciphering their secrets, while improving and making it his own, before turning them back towards their owners.

    Bio[edit]

    No sorcery lies beyond my grasp.
    ▶️  Rubick, the Grand Magus
    Lore:
    Any mage can cast a spell or two, and a few may even study long enough to become a wizard, but only the most talented are allowed to be recognized as a Magus. Yet as with any sorcerer's circle, a sense of community has never guaranteed competitive courtesy.

    Already a renowned duelist and scholar of the grander world of sorcery, it had never occurred to Rubick that he might perhaps be Magus material until he was in the midst of his seventh assassination attempt. As he casually tossed the twelfth of a string of would-be killers from a high balcony, it dawned on him how utterly unimaginative the attempts on his life had become. Where once the interruption of a fingersnap or firehand might have put a cheerful spring in his step, it had all become so very predictable. He craved greater competition. Therefore, donning his combat mask, he did what any wizard seeking to ascend the ranks would do: he announced his intention to kill a Magus.

    Rubick quickly discovered that to threaten one Magus is to threaten them all, and they fell upon him in force. Each antagonist's spell was an unstoppable torrent of energy, and every attack a calculated killing blow. But very soon something occurred that Rubick's foes found unexpected: their arts appeared to turn against them. Inside the magic maelstrom, Rubick chuckled, subtly reading and replicating the powers of one in order to cast it against another, sowing chaos among those who had allied against him. Accusations of betrayal began to fly, and soon the sorcerers turned one upon another without suspecting who was behind their undoing.

    When the battle finally drew to a close, all were singed and frozen, soaked and cut and pierced. More than one lay dead by an ally's craft. Rubick stood apart, sore but delighted in the week's festivities. None had the strength to argue when he presented his petition of assumption to the Hidden Council, and the Insubstantial Eleven agreed as one to grant him the title of Grand Magus.

    Barry Dennen (Responses)

    Details
    • Rubick loses the transformation buff upon losing the Stolen Spell unless explictly stated.
    • Multiple transformation sources' ability effects stack independently of each other. This includes the following ability effects:
      • Attack range definitions.
      • If a transformation source affects illusions, it affects Rubick's illusions as well.
    • Rubick visually transforms to smaller version of the original transformation source, and into the form of the last cast transformation source.
      • Metamorphosis-based abilities have priority over every other transformation sources. Rubick will stay in the demon form until it expires or he dies, regardless of other transformation abilities.
      • Ability particle effects persist and are not overridden.
      • Rubick will use the attack projectile of the last cast transformation source, if the ability expires, then he uses the previous cast one.
    • Other transformation ability notes that are not stated apply.
      • True Form
      • Pierce the Veil
      • Flesh Golem
    • The following ability interactions are exceptions:
      • True Form or Battle Stance together with Flak Cannon or Split Shot causes all the extra projectiles to hit units within range instantly.
      • Despite the visual effects, Elder Dragon Form (Splash Attack, Frost Breath), Arctic Burn, Battle Stance and Shapeshift are applied to the main enemy target only.
        • Secondary targets from Flak Cannon and Split Shot are not hit by those modifiers.

    Trivia[edit]

    • In both DotA and Dota 2, Rubick is the son of Aghanim. He also possesses an imitation of Aghanim's Scepter as one of his cosmetic items.
    • Upon using Walrus PUNCH!, Rubick launches a projectile of himself, spinning and with head ahead, towards the target.
      • The overhead text on targets on hit reasd "Stolen Punch". When the target is Tusk, it reads "Sucker Punch!" instead.
    • Rubick is named after Ernő Rubik, the designer and inventor of the "Magic Cube", commonly known as the "Rubik's Cube")
      • His response, ▶️ "Now, where's my cube?" is a reference to this as well.
      • The comsetics Staff of Perplex, Crux of Perplex, and The Magus Cypher are building into this theme as well.
    • Warcraft 3 similarities:
      • Rubick uses the model of Medivh, a key character within the Warcraft 3 campaign.
      • Fade Bolt is based on the Mountain King's Chain Lightning.
    • Due to engine limitations on Warcraft 3, several spells could not be stolen with Spell Steal.
      • In Warcraft 3, a transformation spell replaces the caster with an alternate version of the caster. As such, they were not usable by Rubick, as it would require a copy of each ability just for Rubick, since the original abilities cannot switch the hero back to Rubick. Although this would be doable in Warcraft with some effort, another unsolvable issue is the stacking of multiple different transformation spells.
      • As a result, Rubick was made unable to steal transformation spells:
        • Elder Dragon Form
        • Shapeshift
        • Metamorphosis
        • Flesh Golem
        • True Form
        • Chemical Rage: Changes in base attack time is only doable via a transformation spell.
        • God's Strength: Switching models (blue model to red model) is only doable via a transformation spell.
        • Flak Cannon: Changes in attack behavior is only doable via a transformation spell.
        • Berserker's Rage: Changes in range type is only doable via a transformation spell.
        • Split Shot: Changes in attack behavior is only doable via a transformation spell.
        • Spirit Form: Switching models (corporeal model to ethereal model) is only doable via a transformation spell.
        • Firefly: Changes in movement type (ground to flying) is only doable via a transformation spell.
        • Arctic Burn: Changes in movement type (ground to flying) is only doable via a transformation spell.
      • Rubick could also not acquire abilities with permanent changes:
        • Attribute Shift
        • Summon Spirit Bear
        • Spin Web
        • Soul Assumption
        • Devour
      • The following abilities could not be acquired for various other reasons, mainly due to engine errors or other coding conflicts:
        • Refraction
        • Borrowed Time
        • Overcharge
        • Spirits
        • Insatiable Hunger
        • Fire Remnant
        • Activate Fire Remnant
        • Rolling Boulder
      • In DotA, Rubick's abilities hotkeys were chosen based on the least-used hotkeys within the game that werent too awkward to use.
      • Since hotkeys could not be customized in Warcraft 3, all spells sharing a hotkey with Rubick's own abilities were unstealable:
        • Abilities with X as their hotkey:
          X Marks the Spot
          Shadowraze (Medium)
          Tornado
          Exorcism
        • Abilities Z as their hotkey:
          Shadowraze (Near)
          Alacrity
        • There were no other abilities with Q as their hotkey.
      • Like in Dota 2, some abilities cannot be stolen because of design reasons, like Invoker's orbs and Invoke, or active attack modifiers.
