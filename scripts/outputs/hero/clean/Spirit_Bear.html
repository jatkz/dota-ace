    A symbiotic companion to its master.
    The one who follows follows the Lone Druid, the Spirit Bear is a powerful companion that Entangles enemies and Demolishes structures. The sound of a Savage Roar is often all that's needed to frighten foes from the forest. If not, then the pair synergies a Spirit Link, increasing their speed and endurance as they roam the lands Hunting for intruders. Man and bear, the Spirit Bear knows its place as Sylla's lifelong companion, symbiotic with his spirit and heart, coming to aid him at any time to fight claw-to-claw alongside his master.

    Aghanim's

    [edit]
    Aghanim's Scepter Upgrade:

    Hero Model

    [edit]
    Hero Model
    Ability
    Passive / Channeling
    Affects
    Self
    Spirit Bear's hero model has the following hidden innate abilities.

    It may only perform or utilize the ability's functions upon a successful channeling, excluding passive innate abilities.
    The Spirit Bear is a Universal Creep-Hero unit with base attribute growth. However, it cannot gain experience.
    Main Attack Damage per Attribute: 0.45
    The Spirit Bear will level up alongside Lone Druid, with its Gold & EXP Bounty scaling as it levels up.

    Most spells and units interact with the Spirit Bear as if it was a Melee Hero.
    Death XP Bounty: 175 + 8
    Death Gold Bounty: 175 + 8
    This ability levels up withLone Druid.
    Details
    Hidden Innate Abilities
    Tap on the innate abilities for more details.
    • Every point of / / attribute grants its respective primary and secondary bonuses.
    • TALENT NOTES
      All Spirit Bear's talents immediately apply to itself once learned.
    • The unit has an inventory and a backpack.
      • Issuing an attack order on an enemy hero will draw the aggro of lane creeps within 500 range of the aggressor hero
      • Can use all item abilities, except planting Observer and Sentry Wards.
      • Can activate all Runes, except the Wisdom Rune.
      • Can interact with Experience Fountain. Pluck Lotus, Lantern of Sight and Watch Tower.
      • Spell damage amplification sources work for the Spirit Bear despite the HUD showing a 0%-value.
      • Consumables work as if a hero is consuming the item.
      • Cannot pick up Aegis of the Immortal.
      • Droppable items upon death works per normal.
    • The cooldown of an item ability is bound to the item itself, and not to the unit wielding the item.
      • If the Spirit Bear has an item on cooldown, and then receives the same item again from Lone Druid but off cooldown, the newly received item is usable right away, despite the other one being still on cooldown.
      • Using the item ability puts it on cooldown and restarts the cooldown of the other one (e.g. Lone Druid and Spirit Bear both having Hand of Midas).
      • However, when an item went on cooldown due to a shared cooldown, it is not on cooldown when given to the Spirit Bear, since the item itself was not used to proc the cooldown.
    • The following item abilities have specific interactions as follows:
      • Combo Breaker's cooldown works completely independently and it is not bound to Lone Druid's.
      • Avatar's duration works completely independently and it is not bound to Lone Druid's.
      • True Sight doesreveal invisible units when wielded by the unit.
        • Reveal can be cast as if a hero is casting the ability.
      • Divine Rapier works as if a hero is wielding the item, and is also muted when given to allied heroes.
        • It is possible to swap an Original Rapier between Lone Druid and the Spirit Bear, as it is not muted like how it is for allies. When picked up by an enemy, it turns into a Free Rapier as usual.
        • The Spirit Bear can pick up Free Rapiers, and just like heroes, it cannot manually drop a Free Rapier from its inventory.
        • However, Free Rapiers cannot be swapped between Lone Druid and the unit like an Original Rapier.

    • Details
    • Interactions
    • Status Effects
    • Show All
    • Requires Aghanim's Shard to be unlocked.
    • Fetch roots the affected target for the debuff duration. The target can still turn, attack, and cast abilities.
      • When the cast range exceeds 425, the target will be teleported 425 distance next to the Spirit Bear, Fetch applies the root debuff and the target is dragged.
    • When the Spirit Bear is affected by other fear sources while active, it moves according to the fear direction.
    • Drags the affected target 425 distance behind the Spirit Bear. The affected target is phased and can be dragged over impassable terrain.
      • The target is moved according to the Spirit Bear's current movement speed.
    • Fetch overrides most position changing effects. Therefore, Fetch has a higher priority on most Forced Movement sources. The following conditions can interrupt Fetch:
      • When either Spirit Bear or target dies.
      • When Spirit Bear teleports a distance greater than 425 range.
      • When the target gets teleported (e.g. Relocate) at any distance.
      • When being affected by same priority abilities: Flaming Lasso, Dismember. Casting these abilities after Fetch will replace Fetch, and vice versa.
      • When being affected by higher priority abilities: Hitch A Ride, Pulverize, Walrus Kick and Arena of Blood.
      • It also breaks if the targets get affected by Fear and Hypnosis sources.
    • Deals 60 magicalspell damage (before reductions) each 0.5-second intervals, starting immediately upon cast, with a total of 5 intervals.
      • Fetch first applies the debuff, then the damage.
    • Cannot be cast on, but can drag invulnerable and hidden units.
    • Each Fetch's instance and duration are independent of each other.
      • Therefore, it is possible to have multiple units grabbed at the same time (e.g. with Refresher Orb).
    Interactions: Debuff Immunity
    The Spirit Bear is still disarmed, and cannot cast abilities, but can use items.
    The debuff fully pierces debuff immunity on affected enemy targets, but they are neither rooted, dragged, or damaged.
