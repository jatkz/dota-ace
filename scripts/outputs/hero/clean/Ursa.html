    Bio[edit]

    It is my spirit that keeps me safe, and not mere armor.
    ▶️  Ulfsaar, the Ursa Warrior
    Alias:
    Ulfsaar
    Lore:
    Ulfsaar the warrior is the fiercest member of an ursine tribe, protective of his land and his people. During the long winters, while the mothers sleep and nurse their cubs, the males patrol the lands above—as tireless, vigilant defenders of their ancient ways. Hearing dim but growing rumors of a spreading evil, Ulfsaar headed out beyond the boundaries of his wild wooded homeland, intending to track down and destroy the threat at its source, before it could endanger his people. He is a proud creature with a bright strong spirit, utterly trustworthy, a staunch ally and defender.
    Voice:
    Fred Tatasciore (Responses)

    Trivia[edit]

    • Ursa does not use his actual name, Ulfsaar, in any of his responses. Lone Druid is the only one who calls him Ulfsaar.
    • In Latin, the word "ursa" means bear. In Portuguese, however, it specifically refers to a female bear. Despite this, Ulfsaar is clearly depicted as male both in his bio (where it is referred to as a "he") and in his in-game dialogue.
    • Ursa's rivalry response towards Batrider▶️ "Batrider, I prevent forest fires." is a reference to the 1940's Anti forest fire mascot "Smokey the Bear".
    • Ursa's death response ▶️ "I join hh-hh the wild spirits." is a reference to the death responses from "Majesty: The Fantasy Kingdom Sim"
    • Fury Swipes may also be a reference to the Pokémon move of the same name. The move involves the target being hit multiple times, much like Ursa's skills allow him to.
    • Warcraft 3 similarities:
      • Ursa uses the model of the Furbolg Trackers, a medium neutral creep.
        • Ursa's title is taken from the Furbolg Ursa Warriors.
      • Earthshock's original iteration was identical to the Mountain King's Thunder Clap.
      • Fury Swipes' original iteration was based on one aspect of the Firelord's Incinerate.
      • Ursa has a 2/13 chance to spawn in with the name "Fuzzy Wuzzy, a reference to the old nursery rhyme, "Fuzzy Wuzzy was a bear".
        • This is referenced in one of his spawning lines, where he calls himself ▶️ "Fuzzy Wuzzy".
