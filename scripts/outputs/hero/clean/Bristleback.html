    Bio[edit]

    It was a barkeep that got me into this mess. Yeah, I think I'll pay em a visit when this is done.
    ▶️  Rigwarl, the Bristleback
    Aliases:
    Rigwarl, BB
    Lore:
    Never one to turn his back on a fight, Rigwarl was known for battling the biggest, meanest scrappers he could get his hands on. Christened Bristleback by the drunken crowds, he waded into backroom brawls in every road tavern between Slom and Elze, until his exploits finally caught the eye of a barkeep in need of an enforcer. For a bit of brew, Bristleback was hired to collect tabs, keep the peace, and break the occasional leg or two (or five, in the case of one unfortunate web-hund).

    After indulging in a night of merriment during which bodily harm was meted out in equal parts upon both delinquent patrons and his own liver, Bristleback finally met his match. "Your tusks offend me, sir," he was heard to drunkenly slur to one particularly large fellow from the northern wastes whose bill had come due. What followed was a fight for the ages. A dozen fighters jumped in. No stool was left unbroken, and in the end, the impossible happened: the tab went unpaid. Over the weeks that followed, Bristleback's wounds healed, and his quills grew back; but an enforcer's honor can be a prickly thing. He paid the tab from his own coin, vowing to track down this northerner and extract redemption. And then he did something he'd never done before: he actually trained, and in so doing made a startling discovery about himself. A smile peeled back from his teeth as he flexed his quills. Turning his back to a fight might be just the thing.

    Snot Rocket
    If Bristleback takes up to a certain amount of damage from the rear, he releases a Viscous Nasal Goo of its corresponding level with this facet selected.
    Effect Radius: 900
    • The ability has three main components:
      • The incoming damage manipulation.
      • The passive ability cast and damage accumulation counter.
      • The active component.
        • The ability compares the incoming damage source's current position and Rigwarl's facing angle per damage instance.
        • With as the centerpoint, its rear as the x-axis, covering a circular segment with an infinite radius.
        • The rear has the following properties:
          • OP and OQ are two rays that defines the rear area, both point and point are positioned 70° clockwise and counterclockwise from point respectively.
            • ∠ POQ = 140°
        • The sides have the following properties:
          • The damage reduction component does not stack with the rear area.
          • Hence, the side's effective area-of-effect is 40° circular segment away from both OP and OQ.
        • Grants Rigwarl the following bonuses within the damage reduction angle:
          • Applies a generic incoming damage reduction on Rigwarl.
            • Stacks additively with other generic incoming damage manipulation source.
            • Does not affect HP Removal sources.
      • The passive ability cast has the following properties:
        • Does not interrupt Rigwarl's channeling abilities.
        • Releases the following abilities of its corresponding level, explicitly when Rigwarl's rear accumulates 275/250/225/200 damage (after all reductions):
          • Quill Spray
          • Viscous Nasal Goo
        • Works independently of the passively released ability's cooldown, and does not cost mana.
        • Does not proc on-cast effects.
      • Grants 1 Warpath stack per passive proc.
        • The damage accumulation counter has the following properties:
          • Has no duration limit, and resets upon passively releasing an ability of the corresponding facet.
          • Accumulates damage sources from all damage types, except from the following sources:
            • HP Removal sources.
            • No-reflection sources.
      • SCEPTER NOTES
        Adds an active component to the Bristleback ability.
        • The ability effects are applied as follows:
          1. Disarms
            • Sets the turn rate, and rotates the hero model 16.67° per server tick until Rigwarl's rear completely faces the point-targeted direction.
            • Neither requires turning nor facing the point-targeted direction to cast the ability.
            • Applies the following ability effects 0.5 seconds after casting:
              • 1 Quill Spray of the corresponding level is released in 0.4-second instances, over 2.4 seconds.
              • Rigwarl's facing angle is now locked. However, he can still move.
            • The active component ends 0.5 seconds after the last Quill Spray released.
            • Turns Rigwarl back to the facing angle before casting if no other orders are issued.
        • Has the following additional properties:
          • Works independently of Quill Spray's cooldown, and does not additionally cost mana.
            • The Quill Spray intervals do not proc on-cast effects
          • bristleback_active_conical_quill_spray
            • The active buff cannot stack and cannot be reapplied.
            • Hence, recasting the active component while the current instance is active causes all ability effects to no longer be applied or happen until the max disarm duration ends.
          • Warpath
            • Grants up to 5 stacks per active cast.
            • Casting the active component itself does not grant stacks.
          • Can only be interrupted by Hex sources.
          • Both hidden and invulnerable sources applied to Rigwarl do not interrupt the active component.
          • Other disable sources applied may change the direction where the Quill Spray instances are released.
      • All Rigwarl's ability casts have the following properties 0.5 seconds after casting:
        • Have an instant cast time.
        • Neither requires turning nor facing the targeted location or unit to cast, as long it is within the cast range.
    Interactions: Self-Illusions
    The incoming damage reduction component is functional.

    However, illusions can neither proc the passive Quill Spray nor Viscous Nasal Goo.

    Trivia[edit]

    • According to an unused voice response, Bristleback was originally designed with tusks.[1]
      • According to Lycan's response ▶️ "A boar is a wolf's natural prey", one of Bristleback's early designs resembled a boar.
    • A silhouette of what appears to be Skywrath Mage can be seen in Bristleback's splash artwork, seemingly still having a beta model/texture.
    • Warcraft 3 similarities:
      • Bristleback is based on the Quillboars, and uses the Razormane Brute model.
        • Quilboars are boar-like humanoids within the Warcraft universe.
        • Bristleback's title is a reference to the Bristleback tribe, a tribe of the Quilboars.
        • Despite this, Bristleback's title was not changed, though some leaks during beta revealed "Bristlebog" as a new title for him.
        • The boar-like features were dropped in Dota 2, likely to distinguish Bristleback from the Quilbaors for copyright reasons.
